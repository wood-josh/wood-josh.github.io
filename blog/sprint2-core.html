Learning Reflection

What is your long-term goal and or career pathway?

As of right now I’m not super sure about the long term. I would like to finish up EDA and probably get a role as a Jr Dev somewhere in Wellington, ideally, as I love this city. What I plan to do in the long term would probably be dependent on what parts of web development I really fall in love with, am thinking that could potentially be video game design or more backend type stuff but am more that ready to have my mind changed!

What skills (non-technical - human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at EDA?

I think having my project management skills developed. And I’m not meaning in the sense of and actual project manager but more in the sense of figuring out where I need to start with this project in order to get to the result that I want. I tend to just kind of jump in a get stuck in with what I think could be a good place to start but often it impacts my productivity which I think could be helpful if that was improved.

A description of your own strengths and limitations and how you will manage the workload?

I think my strengths would be my problem-solving ability, my work ethic and being personable. Limitations would be Time management. Because of these things I know that I need to be strict with myself from day dot of the course starting with putting a plan in place that I will stick to. I think working with team members will be great (as long as my jokes get some laughs), and just continue treating all staff the way I would like to be treated, with respect and patience.

A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way.

I will firstly use the resources given to me, then I will take to the internet, then I will take to slack and utilize the channels I am apart of there, if I still haven’t found and answer to my problem I will get in touch with my facilitator. I also commit to not taking to long on problems where I do get stuck and this can sometimes be and issue of mine and I know I need all the time I can get and can always return back later once the rest of the day’s work is done.

A description of what you expect from the facilitation team

During foundations I don’t expect a lot apart from someone being there if I do need any help with being talked through something I’m really stuck on. Encouragement always goes a long way as well!

Block out times

I don’t really have any commitments that clash with when I’m expecting to be doing this course. The only one would be I pick my cousins up from school on a Wednesday at 3.15 and look after them until 6 but I plan to have the work I wanted done for the day done before I have to go out.
